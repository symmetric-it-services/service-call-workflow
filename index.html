Detailed steps

Step-by-step workflow

1
📋
Manager
Create Service Ticket
Client reports an issue — CCTV offline, network fault, access control failure, fire alarm alert, or any site concern. Manager logs a structured ticket with all required details to ensure no information is missed before dispatch.
Ticket Information to Capture
Client name & full site address
Service category (Networking / CCTV / AV / Fiber / BMS)
Detailed problem description
Urgency level (Critical / High / Medium / Low)
Preferred visit date & time window
Client contact person & phone number
Service Categories
IP Networking & Campus Switches
CCTV & IP Surveillance Systems
Access Control & Fire Alarm
AV / Video Conferencing / Projectors
Fiber Optic & Cabling
Home Automation & BMS
📤 Ticket is saved and a unique Ticket ID is generated. Auto-notification is sent to the team lead immediately upon creation.
2
👤
Manager
Assign Ticket to Field Engineer
Manager reviews the ticket, checks engineer skill match and availability, then assigns the right person. Engineer receives an instant notification with complete ticket details and must accept before dispatch is confirmed.
Assignment Checklist
Match engineer skill to service type
Pick engineer nearest to client site
Confirm engineer availability & schedule
Check required parts & tools availability
Set estimated arrival & resolution time
Engineer Receives
Ticket ID & full problem description
Client address & access instructions
Checklist of tools & parts to carry
Client contact name & phone number
Urgency level & expected resolution time
🔔 Engineer gets push notification + SMS. Acceptance is confirmed before dispatch to avoid delays and miscommunication.
3
📍
Field Engineer
Site Visit, Diagnosis & Repair
Engineer travels to client site, checks in via the system, performs on-site diagnosis, and begins repair or configuration work. Real-time updates are sent to manager and client every 30 minutes throughout the visit.
On-Site Process
Check-in logged with GPS & timestamp
Review ticket & receive client briefing
Diagnose and identify root cause
Photograph fault before starting repair
Perform repair / configuration / installation
Test all systems before wrapping up
Services Handled On-Site
IP Networking, Switches & Routers
CCTV Camera & DVR/NVR Setup
Access Control & Fire Alarm Systems
AV Systems & Video Conferencing
Fiber Optic Splicing & Termination
Home Automation & BMS Integration
🔄 Engineer sends real-time status updates every 30 minutes. Client can also track progress via their Ticket ID through the support portal.
4
✅
Engineer + Manager
Issue Resolved — Ticket Closed
Work is completed, tested, and signed off by the client. Engineer uploads proof of repair and submits a work summary. Manager does final review, approves the closure, and the ticket is marked Resolved in the system.
Engineer Closure Checklist
Before & after photos uploaded to ticket
System tested & fully operational confirmed
Work summary & parts used documented
Client signature or verbal approval recorded
Check-out timestamp logged in system
Manager Final Review
Review engineer's work report & photos
Log client satisfaction rating (1–5 stars)
Flag if a follow-up visit is required
Approve and close ticket in system
Escalate if unresolved within SLA
📧 Ticket status changes to Resolved. Auto-email is sent to client with full work summary, engineer name, and closure confirmation.
5
🧾
Finance / Admin
Invoice Raised & Payment Collected
Finance team generates an itemised invoice based on logged work, parts used, and travel. Invoice is shared with the client via email. Payment is collected, receipt issued, and the ticket is permanently archived.
Invoice Line Items
Labour charges — hours × applicable rate
Spare parts & consumables used on site
Travel / site visit charges
GST / tax breakup as applicable
Total amount due with payment deadline
Accepted Payment Modes
Bank Transfer — NEFT / RTGS / IMPS
UPI / QR Code scan (instant)
Cheque — for corporate / AMC clients
Receipt issued immediately on payment
🔒 Once payment is confirmed, ticket is Final Closed. Record is archived with warranty start date and service history for future reference.

Best practices

Tips for high-speed operations

💬
WhatsApp per ticket
Create a group per ticket — Manager + Engineer + Client. Keeps real-time chat alongside the formal ticket for speed.
📦
Parts inventory tracker
Maintain a live parts inventory so engineers always know availability before heading to site. Reduces repeat visits.
⏱️
SLA timers per urgency
Critical: 4hr, High: same day, Medium: 48hr, Low: 5 days. Set automated reminders so nothing slips through.
⭐
Post-close satisfaction
Send a 1–5 star SMS rating request after every ticket closure. Flags quality issues early and builds client trust.
